What is a good manager? July 29, 2023 by Eduardo Bellani

It is hard to do science without proper definitions. So what is a manager?

Abstracting from (Horstman 2016), my definition of a manager is the following:

A manager balances present output with creating the conditions of future output.

As one can see, this definition is the same as the one for an investor. That is correct. A manager is an investor of company’s resources (human or otherwise). He deploys them with the expectation of generating results in the present. If he is good, he deploys them in such a way as to create conditions to generate more results in the future.

As an illustration, a manager can usually deliver more results in the present by burning up people. It probably will increase turnover in the future. Is it the right choice? Only with a holistic view and sound judgement can one decide correctly.

Given this definition, one can easily see that a good manager is not some things:

  • An extrovert
  • A good communicator
  • A frequent yeller
  • Someone who cares
  • Someone who is agreeable

PS: A complicating factor for the manager is that he is trying to balance a system over time, but the balancing point itself changes.

References

Horstman, M. 2016. The Effective Manager. Wiley. https://www.wiley.com/en-us/The+Effective+Manager-p-9781119244608.
